Reliable skipped instantly; Brilliant near end; WC 2-3 and 3-4 mid-timer.
Two distinct skip behaviours emerge:
— Reliable (6hr): Median skip at 100% of timer — players skip the entire thing from the start. Treated as an instant action, not a timed wait. Tight HC distribution (q25–q75 = 48–72 HC) confirms everyone's paying the same flat cost.
— Brilliant & WC 1→2 (24–72hr): Median at 5–7% of timer — almost all the waiting is done, HC cost is minimal. A patience-completion nudge, not a skip.
— WC 2→3 & 3→4 (120–180hr): Median at 25–40%, patience breaks noticeably earlier. q90 hits 100% for both — bimodal group: some wait till the end, others give up mid-way. These are your highest HC-per-skip events (median 144–281 HC).

Waited Skipped
Gap to next star-up is similar for skipped and waited groups.
Skipping doesn't meaningfully accelerate the next star-up. The gap between completing a star-up and starting the next one is nearly identical for skippers and waiters across all types. For WC 3→4, skippers actually wait slightly longer (82hr vs 74hr). The bottleneck post star-up is SUT balance and player availability — not the timer. This rules out timer reduction as a progression unlocker.

Avg daily PvP matches — pre baseline vs during queue (3-day window)
Pre (baseline) During queue
WC types show clear engagement dip; Reliable and Brilliant flat or up.
Engagement cost scales monotonically with WC tier. Reliable and Brilliant users play the same or slightly more during queue — the timer is too short to affect lineup and these users are in their naturally high-activity early journey. WC 1→2 through WC 3→4 show a clean, escalating dip: -4.5%, -8.7%, -11.6%. WC 3→4 users are your most active (8.7 matches/day pre) and drop by ~1 match/day for up to 7.5 days — the only tier where there's a genuine engagement-based argument for timer reduction.
Reliable & Brilliant 1→2 — no cost
Timer too short to feel. Star-up initiation is part of an active session. Bench depth covers absence easily. During engagement up slightly — session effect, not star-up effect.
Brilliant 2→3 & WC 1→2 — mild
Inflection point. Player begins to matter to the lineup but bench can still cover. Dip is real (-1.8% to -4.5%) but small enough that it's not a priority concern.
WC 2→3 & WC 3→4 — meaningful
These players are irreplaceable in the active lineup. Long timers (5–7.5 days) compound the absence. Engagement dip is sustained and scales with rarity. Only tier where a system change is justified on engagement grounds.
