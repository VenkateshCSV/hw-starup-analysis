$51–$100 tier: 145 users, 44.4% of Q90 payer revenue (₹2.67L). Most disproportionate tier in the entire dataset. ARPPU of ₹8,919 — surfacing M2+ SP directly to these users at reactivation is the single highest-ROI action.
>$100 tier: 50% D14 retention and 25.7% SP conv — both the highest of any tier. SP D0 is already 9.3%, meaning ~1 in 10 buy a subscription on reactivation day itself. No nudge needed — these users come back with clear intent.
Irregular SP is most effective in the $1–$25 range (+8.6pp D0→D14). These users had a real but modest spend habit, lapsed long enough to qualify, and respond meaningfully. The $26–$50 tier shows even steeper growth (+13.5pp) as the top of the Irregular SP ceiling.
<$1 tier: only +1.74pp SP growth across 14 days despite Irregular SP eligibility. Micro-spenders technically qualify but aren't responding. They converted before at trivially low amounts — subscription propensity is low regardless of what's served. Low investment priority.
Offer preference maps perfectly to spend tier. Low spenders → social/utility (Rent a Player, Match Replay). Mid spenders → Match Replay + FTB/Irregular SPs. High spenders → subscription packs (M2+ SP, M2+ SP Gold) without needing any discount. Serving the right offer tier at reactivation is a direct revenue lever.

04 — Opportunities

Where to focus for Q90

Actions directly supported by the data above.

🎯

Identify returning payers at install

36.3% of Q90 users are previous payers driving 91% of Q90 revenue. Flagging them at reactivation enables a dedicated flow — different offer, different onboarding. Currently treated identically to non-payers.

💳

Tier-aware offer surfacing

The $51–$100 tier (145 users) generates ₹2.67L — 44% of Q90 payer revenue. Surfacing M2+ SP immediately to users identified in this tier could meaningfully lift conversion, given their D0 SP intent.

▶️

Match Replay as a payer re-entry hook

Match Replay is the #1 offer for returning payers (34.4%) and #2 for the $1–$25 tier (43.3%). Returning payers want continuity, not a fresh start. A "here's what happened while you were away" moment at reactivation could be a high-conversion entry point.

📉

Deprioritise <$1 tier for SP investment

<$1 tier is 55.5% of returning payers but only 5.3% of Q90 payer revenue. Irregular SP is available to them but barely moves the needle (+1.74pp D0→D14). Marginal SP investment here has poor returns — effort is better directed at the $1–$50 Irregular SP window.
